It's frightening how simple it is yet how many struggle profoundly with it.

We have a set of tasks we could do to get where we want to be, and become who we want to become.

Most people know what this set of things is. At the very least, they know the first step. It can be as simple as cleaning your room.

But many of us exist in an anxious paralysis, not making progress, but not enjoying the present moment. Our time, the most finite and precious thing in the universe, goes utterly wasted.

For most people, the problem frighteningly simple. Its that we are primitive dopamine driven animals existing in a hyper modernized world.

Who honestly wants a salad after donuts for breakfast? But have you honestly tried a spinach salad with herbs, lemon, avocado, black pepper, after fasting for 16 hours?

Who honestly wants to look at an excel spreadsheet, code a website, start an online business after watching tiktok for 8 hours.

Who honestly wants to deal with fucking life when smoking weed and playing video games is an impulse away.

If we choose to let our biological impulses dictate our actions, we are doomed to fail.

Design your life with this in mind.

Keep your enemies out of your home. Don't have donuts in your kitchen. Don't invest in a gamer lair that only exists to escape life and feel like shit afterwards.

We can make it actually fun to spend the bulk of our time making progress (commonly referred to as "working"). We just cut out all the other bullshit, then the only thing left is making progress, and enjoying the present moment.

Just stop escaping your life, and remove the triggers that keep you in that loop.

Instead, use your finite time making progress. Watch the results accumulate, and earn confidence that you can do great things.

Don't forget to enjoy life in the meantime. We only have one, and much like we can't rush losing weight, we can't rush success. Don't rush to the finish line of life (AKA death). Enjoy the race, get in a groove, feel the breeze, feel the presence of others, enjoy the sun.

And do your best.
